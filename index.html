I build design systems that eliminate friction between design and development. With 20 years bridging strategy, design, and engineering, I create scalable component libraries, establish design token architectures, and implement workflows that help teams ship faster without compromising quality. I lead by making complex systems accessible. From establishing governance frameworks to mentoring engineers and prototyping new patterns, my focus is removing obstacles so teams can do their best work. I bring deep expertise in React, TypeScript, and Figma combined with strategic thinking about how design systems serve business goals.

About me

My story

My Experience

Education

Certifications

I'm a design systems engineer, originally from Venezuela and French citizen since 2015, based in the Netherlands. I've spent the last 20 years building bridges between design and engineering, working across Europe and Latin America for companies ranging from startups to enterprise.


My background is unconventional. I started in Business Process consulting, moved into Product Development, and eventually found my home at the intersection of Design and Code as a Design Engineer.


Outside of work, I volunteer with electronic music festivals, handling websites, mobile apps and mailing campaigns. I love to travel and I'm trilingual (Spanish, French, English), which has shaped how I think about communication and collaboration across cultures.

Design tokens

SCSS architecture

Team leadership

How I work

Answers that move things forward

Design engineering is about bridging vision and execution. I approach projects systematically, align stakeholders, collaborate across functions, and mentor teams to build sustainable systems. Here's my process and philosophy.

I start with discovery, not solutions

Every project begins with understanding the full landscape: business goals, user needs, technical constraints, and team dynamics. I invest time upfront interviewing stakeholders, auditing existing systems, and mapping pain points before proposing solutions. This research phase prevents building the wrong thing and creates buy-in from day one.
